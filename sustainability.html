Sustainability

Property management
that does more good.

We believe property management has a real role to play in changing how buildings are run — by making responsible choices easy, visible, and normal for owners, tenants, and the vendors who serve them.

We are not asking anyone to transform overnight. We are building simple, practical habits into the way properties are managed — reducing waste, protecting resources, and creating healthier spaces for people to live and work in.

Our Approach

Small decisions. Made consistently. At scale.

Every property on the Javen platform is an opportunity to do things a little better than before. Our sustainability approach is not built on grand promises — it is built into the day-to-day operations of every property we manage.

From the vendors we work with to the way tenants interact with their homes, we embed responsible habits into the fabric of property management in Tanzania.

The nine pillars below describe the sustainability commitments Javen actively promotes across all managed properties, vendors, and tenants.

"Sustainability in Tanzania is not about grand gestures — it is about consistent, small decisions made by many people. Javen exists to make those decisions easier for every property owner and tenant we serve."

— JAVEN, Founded 2026, Dar es Salaam

What We Actively Do

Our Nine Sustainability Pillars

The following nine pillars describe the sustainability commitments Javen promotes across all managed properties, vendors, and tenants.

Pillar 01
Going Paperless
Leases, maintenance requests, rent receipts, and notices are all handled digitally on Javen — significantly cutting the paper used in day-to-day property management.
Pillar 02
Eco-Friendly Vendors
We encourage all cleaning and maintenance vendors on our platform to use biodegradable, eco-friendly products that are safe for people and the environment.
Pillar 03
Green Building Standards
We work with property owners to adopt energy-saving practices — cross-ventilation, insulation, LED lighting, and solar installations — reducing electricity consumption and costs.
Pillar 04
Waste Separation
We promote waste sorting at the property level — separating food waste, plastics, and general rubbish — making recycling possible and reducing what ends up in landfill.
Pillar 05
Reducing Plastic Waste
Through our vendor and tenant guidelines, we actively discourage single-use plastics and support alternatives — from water refill systems to reusable cleaning supplies.
Pillar 06
Green Spaces
We encourage property owners to incorporate gardens, trees, and landscaping — improving air quality, reducing heat, and making properties more attractive and liveable.
Pillar 07
Water Conservation
We guide owners toward water-saving fixtures, rainwater harvesting tanks, and responsible irrigation — critical in a city where water access is not always guaranteed.
Pillar 08
Preventive Maintenance
Fixing problems early extends the life of buildings and appliances, reducing the waste and cost that comes from emergency replacements and neglected repairs.
Pillar 09
Awareness & Education
We share practical sustainability tips with tenants and owners through the Javen platform — making responsible living accessible to everyone, not just those already aware.

Global Standards

UN Sustainable Development Goals We Support

Javen actively contributes to six UN SDGs through the day-to-day operation of our platform and the standards we set for properties, vendors, and tenants across Tanzania.

SDG 3
Good Health & Well-Being
Eco-friendly cleaning products and green spaces create healthier homes and workplaces for tenants and their families.
SDG 7
Affordable & Clean Energy
We promote solar panels, LED lighting, and energy-efficient building designs to reduce reliance on the grid and lower energy costs.
SDG 11
Sustainable Cities & Communities
Every green building and well-managed property contributes to a safer, cleaner, and more liveable Dar es Salaam for everyone.
SDG 12
Responsible Consumption & Production
Waste separation, plastic reduction, and eco-vendor standards directly support more responsible patterns of consumption across our portfolio.
SDG 13
Climate Action
Reducing energy use, cutting paper, and encouraging green infrastructure are concrete steps toward lowering our collective carbon footprint in Tanzania.
SDG 17
Partnerships for the Goals
We work with vendors, owners, tenants, and local stakeholders to build a shared culture of responsibility across the Tanzanian property sector.
